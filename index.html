The honest read most lease pages won't give you

03Which fits you

Lease vs. finance: which fits you?

There's no single right answer, it depends entirely on how you use a vehicle. We run both sets of numbers for every Sequoia shopper, side by side, so the decision is based on math you can see rather than a sales pitch.

Lease the Sequoia if you:

  • Want the lowest possible monthly payment for a given trim.
  • Like driving a new vehicle every two to three years.
  • Drive a predictable number of miles each year (most leases run 10,000–15,000 annually).
  • Prefer staying under manufacturer warranty the entire time you have the vehicle.
  • Don't want to deal with reselling or trading in down the road.

Finance the Sequoia if you:

  • Plan to keep the SUV well past the loan payoff.
  • Drive high mileage and don't want allowance limits hanging over you.
  • Want to build equity and eventually own the vehicle outright.
  • Like the freedom to modify, tow heavy year-round, or sell whenever you choose.
Not every customer who walks in asking to lease should lease, and we'll say so. We'd rather tell you that before you sign than collect a return fee in three years.

Here's the contrarian point we make to a lot of Sequoia shoppers: if you tow at the upper end of that 9,500-lb rating regularly, or you rack up 18,000+ miles a year running the freeways between National City, Chula Vista, and downtown San Diego, leasing is probably the wrong tool. Mileage overages and heavy-use wear at lease-end can erase the monthly savings. Not sure which way to go? Reach out and we'll get you both quotes in writing so you can compare real numbers, not guesses.

04The numbers

How Sequoia lease pricing actually works.

A lease payment isn't a mystery, even though plenty of dealers treat it like one. Three things drive it, and we'll show you each one.

1. The capitalized cost

Essentially the negotiated price of the Sequoia. A lower agreed price means a lower payment, which is why negotiating the price still matters on a lease, not just the monthly figure.

2. The residual value

Toyota's projection of what the Sequoia will be worth at lease-end. Full-size Toyotas historically hold value well, and a strong residual is good for you: it means less depreciation to pay for across the term.

3. The money factor

The lease equivalent of an interest rate, tied to your credit and to current Toyota Financial Services programs.

If a fee doesn't belong, ask, we'll explain every one. We itemize them on your quote rather than rolling them into a vague "drive-off" lump.

What we do before we hand you a number

When you ask us for a Sequoia lease quote, our team pulls the current Toyota Financial Services residual and money-factor program for the exact trim and term, then verifies it against the live capitalized cost on the in-stock unit. On the last Capstone we leased, we pulled the residual, checked the money factor against the customer's credit tier, and confirmed the drive-off line by line before anything was signed. We've done that thousands of times across the brand's lineup, and we'll show you each line so you can see where the payment comes from.

A note on fees: every lease has them, acquisition fees, your first month's payment, taxes, registration, and a security-related disposition fee if you return the vehicle at the end. We itemize these on your quote rather than rolling them into a vague "drive-off" lump. If you ever see a charge you don't understand on a Dalton lease worksheet, that's a question we want you to ask, not a number we want you to skip past.

End of lease: your options at Dalton.

One of the questions we get most is "what happens when the lease is up?" The good news is you have choices, and none of them are sprung on you at the last minute. As your term winds down, our team reaches out so there's time to plan.

Turn it in and lease something new

Drop the keys and walk into a fresh Sequoia or a different Toyota, with a new term structured around how your driving has changed.

Buy it out

Purchase your Sequoia at the residual price already set in your contract, which can be a smart move if the vehicle's market value is higher than the buyout.

Walk away

Return the vehicle, settle any mileage or excess-wear charges, and you're done.

08Sequoia lease questions

Frequently asked questions about Toyota Sequoia lease deals.

How much does it cost to lease a Toyota Sequoia?

Your monthly payment depends on the trim, term length, annual mileage, and down payment you choose, plus the current Toyota Financial Services residual and money factor for that exact build.

Because every lease is built around those variables, the only honest way to get an accurate number is a custom quote, request one at toyotadalton.com or call 619-474-5573 and we'll put it in writing the same day.

Can I lease a Sequoia with no money down?

Lease structures with little or no money down are often possible, but here's the trade-off most ads skip: a lower down payment means a higher monthly payment, because you're financing more of the cost across the term.

Our team will show you both versions side by side so you can pick what actually fits your budget rather than chasing one number on a billboard.

Is the Sequoia a hybrid?

Yes. The current-generation Sequoia uses Toyota's i-FORCE MAX hybrid powertrain across every trim, a twin-turbo V6 paired with an electric motor producing a combined 437 horsepower and 583 lb-ft of torque, per Toyota's official specifications at toyota.com.

The EPA-rated combined fuel economy published at fueleconomy.gov lands around 22 MPG for the rear-drive version, efficient for a full-size SUV this capable.

How much can a Toyota Sequoia tow?

Properly equipped, the 2026 Sequoia is rated to tow up to 9,500 lbs according to Toyota's official figures at toyota.com.

If you regularly tow near that ceiling, mention it when you contact us, heavy towing affects whether leasing or financing is the better call for you, and we'd rather flag that before you sign than after.

Is the Sequoia a safe vehicle for my family?

Every 2026 Sequoia comes standard with Toyota Safety Sense, the suite of pre-collision braking, lane-departure alert, and adaptive cruise control.

For independent crash-test results, we point families to the National Highway Traffic Safety Administration at nhtsa.gov and the Insurance Institute for Highway Safety at iihs.org, read those yourself rather than taking any dealer's word for it.

What happens at the end of my Sequoia lease?

You'll typically have three options: return the vehicle and lease something new, buy out your Sequoia at the residual price already set in your contract, or walk away after settling any mileage or excess-wear charges.

We reach out well before the term ends so you have time to choose, and we tell you the mileage and wear rates at signing so nothing surprises you at the curb.

Does Dalton Toyota National City offer Sequoia finance options too?

Yes. If buying makes more sense for how you drive, high mileage, heavy towing, or a plan to keep the SUV long term, we'll structure a finance offer alongside your lease quote so you can compare both side by side.

Just ask when you reach out through toyotadalton.com or call 619-474-5573.
